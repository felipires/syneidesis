# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single author — the maker, Felipe — writing for himself first. Context: quiet,
reflective moments (morning pages, late-night thoughts, working through an idea),
across devices (laptop and phone), often **offline**. The job to be done is to get
what's in his head onto the page with as little friction as possible — journaling
daily entries and writing standalone articles — and occasionally to **share** a
finished piece publicly, read-only. Readers of public pieces are secondary: they
arrive via a shared link or the public index and simply read.

## Product Purpose

Syneidesis is a personal writing space — a journal + article webapp reachable
everywhere, offline-first. It exists to make reflection a habit: a calm digital
place to externalize thoughts, keep dated journals, and refine loose articles.
The deeper intent (from the project's philosophy) is reverence for discovery —
that every concept is "a small universe" worth rediscovering, and that the act of
writing brings light to the mind. Success = the author actually writes in it,
regularly, because opening it feels like sitting down with a good notebook rather
than logging into a tool.

## Brand Personality

Calm, contemplative, intimate. Three words: **quiet, luminous, unhurried.** The
voice is gentle and personal, never corporate or gamified. The interface should
feel like fine paper and ink — the writing is the only thing that matters; the app
disappears around it. Emotional goal: stillness and focus, a small sanctuary. Light
"emerges" through the act of writing rather than being shouted by the UI.

## Anti-references

Explicitly should NOT feel like:
- **Corporate SaaS / Notion** — busy sidebars, dense toolbars, productivity-tool
  coldness, generic dashboard energy.
- **Social media** — feeds, metrics, likes, notifications, infinite scroll, any
  attention-grabbing mechanics.
- **Trendy AI-startup aesthetics** — gradients, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark,
  cream/sand "editorial" clichés, loud hero pages.
- **Cluttered / decorative** — ornamentation for its own sake, competing elements,
  visual noise.

## Design Principles

1. **The writing is the interface.** Chrome recedes; words lead. When in doubt,
   remove. The editor especially should feel like an empty, inviting page.
2. **Calm over capable.** Favor stillness and a single clear action per moment over
   feature density. No badges, counters, or dopamine hooks.
3. **Light emerges.** Meaning and emphasis surface through the act of writing and
   reading — typography, space, and restraint — not through decoration.
4. **Trustworthy and always-there.** Offline-first and autosaving; the app never
   loses words and never blocks on the network. Quiet reliability is part of the calm.
5. **Two lenses, one soul.** Journal (dated, intimate) and collections/articles
   (curated) share one calm system; the public reader inherits the same restraint.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (no light-gray-for-elegance), large
text ≥3:1, full keyboard operation, visible focus states, readable type sizes, and
`prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation. Long-form legibility is a first-
class concern (comfortable measure, line-height, and type scale for sustained reading).
